Downtime

To add more of a mechanical weight to downtime, this game is using a system similar to the downtime mechanic from Blades in the Dark

Between major events, your group will enter downtime. During this phase, we use clocks with 4, 6, or 8 sides to represent activities and improvements you're working towards. For each downtime phase, you have two downtime actions to spend. Spending an activity will fill up one section of a clock. You can spend 1 barter per additional downtime action an unlimited number of times.

While you're at war, you only have one downtime action to spend.

Clocks

Clocks are a visual representation of time or effort, using a circle divided into different sections, as shown below. You can use checkboxes, a progress bar, or any other visual representation, but clocks are just an easy way to understand it at a glance.

You can have a total of 3 downtime clocks running at a time. You may have less if you'd like; the Savvyhead specifically may have more. In war, you must choose to freeze one of those clocks until war is over, as your attention can only be so divided. For each downtime action you spend, you will fill up one section of the relevant clock. Your options for clocks include:

  • Run a gig (no clock, see below)
  • Add a tag to weapon or equipment (4-sided)
  • Create, repair, or find an item (6-sided)
  • Train towards an advancement (8-sided)

These timings may be adjusted depending on the MoC's discretion. For example, repairing a Golden Age weapon with rare parts may take an 8-sided clock rather than a 6-sided clock.

Gigs

Most have to work to stay alive out here. Gigs generally do not last longer than one downtime period. Because of this, clocks are unnecessary. Instead, you'll search for work and the MoC will describe the job. You will describe how you go about that job, and it may require a roll or a series of rolls, depending on complexity. Barter will be rewarded accordant to the rolls and the job being done.
